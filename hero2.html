Kathmandu Living Labs

Six months have passed since the devastating Nepal earthquake. In that time, Kathmandu Living Labs has taken a key role in mapping and information management in support of the relief effort. We have designed solutions and created maps that communities, individuals, the government, and NGOs use for relief and recovery.

We're proud to show a small sampling of what we have accomplished in theses six months.

Responding to the Nepal earthquake...

QuakeMap
QuakeMap was the centerpiece of Kathmandu Living Labs’ response to the Nepal Earthquake. A web-based mapping and reporting platform, QuakeMap connects earthquake victims with organizations and government agencies that can provide aid. Deployed the day after the earthquake, QuakeMap users created reports, and volunteers tracked the reports until aid arrived.


Mobilizing Mapping Volunteers

At the time of the earthquake, OpenStreetMap’s coverage of rural Nepal was nearly nonexistent. Kathmandu Living Labs worked with nearly 9,000 international mappers from the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team to use aerial imagery in order to fill in the map of Nepal. The total amount of data on the OSM Nepal map increased ten times.

QuakeRelief

While online maps like OpenStreetMap are crucial in disaster situations, relief workers in the field needed detailed, printed maps. Kathmandu Living Labs responded by building a repository of printable maps using the OpenStreetMap data created by mapping volunteers. These maps cover affected districts and villages and were a vital resource for relief workers, especially when and where internet access was limited.

Visualizing Development
KLL and Google collaborated on a map-based visualization of Nepal’s development indicators, such as literacy rate and life expectancy. The visualization provides an easy-to-comprehend tool, and shows surprising statistics, such as that Manang, home of the Annapurna Circuit, has the highest income per capita. KLL worked closely with National Planning Commission on this project. Read More

Training Transit Engineers

Building on its commitment to community mapping trainings, several members of KLL traveled through Nepal to train engineers in collecting road network data. The data collected on road type and road condition is crucial for development, as it allows Nepal to create good maps of road networks, make infrastructure investment decisions, and manage maintenance of the existing road network.

Food Security Mapping

Kathmandu Living Labs worked with Small Farmers Cooperative to train and engage local entrepreneurs, farmers, and school students to map agriculture and food security data in OpenStreetMap. The project took advantage of the participants’ local knowledge to map agricultural projects, and then make the data freely available.

From the director

The Nepal earthquake was my first experience of major disaster. This disaster has taught me many things and pushed our entire team to devote their energy to accomplishing things that seem impossible.

We have so many people to thank in the wake of the earthquake. A team of local volunteers assisted in our work, and our mapping efforts were made possible by additional volunteers around the world. In addition, Humanity United and Humanity Road are just two of the many organizations that made our work possible. After the earthquake, our office was destroyed and we were forced to work without an office, but Buzz Cafe and Kasthamandap School Last but not least, I would like to recognize the Kathmandu Living Labs team members for their work in such a difficult time.

Finally, the reconstruction is a longer-term process, which is very information intensive. Nepal’s geography will rapidly change in reconstruction—new buildings and new construction are part of the recovery. KLL will do whatever it can in this ongoing process with our skills in information technology. While we face the challenge of assisting the recovery, we look forward to helping rebuild.
